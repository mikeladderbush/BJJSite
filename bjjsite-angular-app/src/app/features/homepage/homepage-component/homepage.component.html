UNITED AS A FAMILY

Bill Duffy Bill Duffy is a Brazilian Jiu Jitsu instructor with over two decades of martial arts experience, currently teaching at Kimura BJJ in Methuen, MA. After a distinguished career in law enforcement, serving as a police officer for 20 years, and an earlier stint in the U.S. Marine Corps from 1980 to 1987, Bill shifted his focus toward his passion for Brazilian Jiu Jitsu. His journey in BJJ began in 2002 after a grappling competition sparked his desire to truly master the art. Although he won the beginner division of his first Gi competition without prior training in the Gi, a broken arm in the advanced division made him realize the importance of formal training. The following week, Bill committed himself to learning BJJ, and his dedication to the art has only grown since. Training under Master Jean Kleber at Kimura BJJ, Bill traveled over 100 miles round trip to train, balancing family life, a demanding job, and persistent injuries, all driven by his deep love for the art. His commitment to BJJ was further solidified when he attended the 2005 Gracie Cruise, where he had the honor of learning from Grandmaster Helio Gracie himself. Bill was deeply inspired by Helio's agility on the mat, even at the age of 93, and it further fueled his passion for training. In 2010, Bill co-founded Kimura BJJ Tewksbury with his friend and fellow instructor Fabiano, with the goal of sharing the benefits of BJJ with the community. A certified defensive tactics instructor, Bill brings his extensive experience from both law enforcement and martial arts into his teaching. He emphasizes the importance of mastering the basics, believing that a strong foundation is essential for growth in the art. His classes are designed to build skills step-by-step, from beginners to advanced students, ensuring that everyone gains a deep understanding of BJJ. Although Bill has competed in and won many tournaments over the past decade, his focus has shifted to teaching. He believes that his experience as a competitor has significantly enhanced his teaching, allowing him to pass on valuable insights to his students. Bill continues to train daily, relying on "lots of Advil and ice packs" to stay healthy.
